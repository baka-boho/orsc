ABOUT US

Who We Are

Operations Research Society is one of the most notable scientific clubs in the university of Science & Technology Houari Boumediene in Algeria and the first one to be specialized in the field of Mathematics, more precisely in Operations Research

Our History

It was first created on May 2nd 1990 under the name "Club Recherche op√©rationnelle" accumulating several awards & distinctions such as the 2nd best club in our university in 2016. In 2019 it was relaunched by a new team and rebranded as ORSC with a new identity and ambition to tackle topical challenges

Our Mission

Operations Research Society represents the community of OR enthusiasts in Algeria and looks to promote this discipline and show its importance in different sectors, while linking students to domain experts. Today the club's evolution continues to achieve its mission through diverse activities and events.

OUR PROJECTS

TSP Web App

A web app that introduces the Traveling Salesman Problem by providing users with the ability to attempt solving an instance of the problem with a set of Algerian wilayas (cities) and compare their result with that of the genetic algorithm.

Try It GitHub

Knapsack Web App

A web app that introduces the Knapsack Problem by providing users with the ability to attempt solving an instance of the problem and compare their result with that of the branch and bound method.

Try It GitHub

OR Game

A desktop multilevel game where players go through a series of challenges based on problems in Operations Research. The first level is based on the Knapsack problem where the player picks items needed for a camping trip.

Play Now GitHub

Whale Optimization Algorithm

A research project exploring the Whale Optimization Algorithm (WOA), a nature-inspired metaheuristic optimization algorithm that is designed to solve complex optimization problems. The algorithm simulates how whales search for and attack prey to converge on an optimal solution.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Registrations are open year round, you can join Operations Research Society Club by following this form link.

What we care about most at ORSC is to bring aboard motivated people who are enthusiastically curious about OR and are looking to learn and work on cool events and projects within the club.

All motivated people are welcome, the only requirement is to be a university student.

As a member of ORSC you'll work on projects and events related to OR, perform tasks within your club department, learn new things in workshops and training, and make new friends and acquaintances.
